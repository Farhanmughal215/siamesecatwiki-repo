About

An independent reference
about Siamese cats.

siamese.wiki is a free encyclopedia about one very specific thing — the Siamese cat. Thirty long-form articles, cited from veterinary journals, breed registries, and primary historical sources.

Why this exists

This wiki is sponsored by Siamese Cat Café, a cat café in Bangna, Bangkok with sixteen rescued Siamese in residence. The café wanted a trustworthy English-language reference to point people to — one that would survive a pub-quiz check by a vet or a breeder. So we wrote one.

The editorial relationship is simple: the café pays the hosting bill and gets a small, honest mention at the top and bottom of every article. It does not influence content, and no article has been commissioned to promote the café or any product. We don't take affiliate links. We don't run ads. We don't accept paid placements from breeders or pet-food companies.

How we write

  • Every non-trivial claim is sourced. Citations are listed at the bottom of each article. We prioritize peer-reviewed veterinary journals, breed registry standards (CFA, TICA, GCCF), and university vet schools.
  • Health articles carry disclaimers. We describe conditions; we don't prescribe treatment. Anything serious, we tell you to see a vet.
  • We take positions. When sources converge, we say what's true. When they don't, we explain the disagreement instead of sitting on the fence.
  • We flag uncertainty. "Estimates vary" means the data is actually inconsistent. We'd rather admit that than invent precision.

What we don't do

  • We don't write about the café's resident cats by name. Their stories live at siamesecat.cafe.
  • We don't recommend specific breeders. Ask local breed-rescue organizations.
  • We don't run product reviews. "Best food for Siamese" articles from sites with affiliate links are almost never honest.
  • We don't use AI to generate content. Articles are drafted and edited by humans, with AI used only as a research and copy-editing tool.
